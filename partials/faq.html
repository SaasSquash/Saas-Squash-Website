FAQ

Questions Operators Ask Before They Engage.

Straight answers—no buzzwords. If something here does not match your environment, say so on the intro call and we will adjust scope.

Do you need access to our ERP or customer data?

Not always. Assessments often start with interviews, sample exports, and anonymized workflows. When we integrate, we scope read-only paths first and document every connection before agents touch production data.

Who from our side should be in the room?

A sponsor with budget authority (owner, COO, or VP Ops), someone who knows how work actually gets done (sales or branch leadership), and a technical counterpart (IT or systems admin). Legal or compliance joins when governance touches customer or financial data.

Local models vs. cloud LLMs—which do you recommend?

It depends on sensitivity and task type. Repetitive internal drafting, classification, and account prep often run well on local models when context is pre-loaded. Frontier APIs make sense for complex reasoning—when you have approved data paths and cost controls. We help you draw that line explicitly.

What about IP and confidentiality?

Your file architecture, skill documents, and agent definitions are yours. We do not claim ownership of your business content. Contract terms spell out confidentiality, subprocessors (if any), and what happens at project end.

How is this different from buying another AI SaaS product?

Rented tools optimize for recurring revenue and generic workflows. We help you build durable structure—files, skills, and agents—that compounds in value and does not reset when a vendor changes pricing or features.

What happens after the assessment if we do not continue?

You keep the scorecard, matrix, roadmap, and governance outline. Many clients execute internally; others return for a focused build phase. There is no lock-in to a platform subscription from us.
