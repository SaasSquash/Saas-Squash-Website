What You Get From an AI Readiness Assessment.

Most AI initiatives stall before production—not because the models are weak, but because governance, data, and ownership were never defined. We start with a structured assessment so you know where you stand and what to build first, before you buy another subscription.

How we score readiness

01

Governance & alignment

Executive sponsorship, acceptable use, risk appetite, and who owns decisions when agents touch customer or financial data.

02

Data & context

Where knowledge lives today, quality and access patterns, and whether your files can support agents without re-explaining every session.

03

Architecture & tools

ERP, CRM, identity, and integration reality—what can connect safely and what should stay on local or air-gapped paths.

04

People & adoption

Who will maintain the system, how reps and operators will interact with agents, and what training gaps exist.

05

Value & ROI

Workflows ranked by manual cost and feasibility—so investment maps to processes you can measure, not science projects.

Tangible outputs

  • AI readiness scorecard across the five pillars above, with plain-language gaps
  • Prioritized use-case matrix — impact, effort, and dependency order
  • 12–18 month roadmap aligned to your capacity, not a vendor product calendar
  • Governance starter kit — roles, escalation paths, and acceptable-use themes you can refine with counsel
  • Executive readout — board-ready summary with ROI framing against manual work replaced
  • Architecture sketch for an intelligent file layout and agent boundaries in your environment

Typical assessment: 4–6 weeks, co-designed with your IT and operations leads. No obligation to continue into build or enablement afterward.
